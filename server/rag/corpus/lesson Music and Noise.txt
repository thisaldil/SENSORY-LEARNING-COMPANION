SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: C1
NAME: Sound as Vibrations
DEFINITION:
All sounds are produced by vibrations, but the brain identifies them differently based on vibration pattern and effect on listeners.

KEY_POINTS:

Sound is created when objects or air particles vibrate.
Both music and noise come from vibrations.
Our brain classifies sound by pattern and by how it feels to us.

EXAMPLES:
A drum skin vibrates to produce sound, but the sound may be musical or noisy depending on vibration pattern.

KEYWORDS:
sound, vibration, brain processing, auditory perception, wave pattern

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: C2
NAME: Music as Regular Vibrations
DEFINITION:
Music is sound that is usually pleasant and is produced by regular, repeating vibrations.

KEY_POINTS:

Musical sounds are periodic and organized.
Regular vibration creates clear musical notes.
Music often produces calm or happy feelings in listeners.

EXAMPLES:
The rhythmic beat of a Sabaragamuwa drum or the steady note of a bamboo flute sounds musical because vibration is regular.

KEYWORDS:
music, regular vibration, periodic wave, musical note, rhythm

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: C3
NAME: Noise as Irregular Vibrations
DEFINITION:
Noise is sound that is unpleasant, harsh, or disturbing and is produced by irregular, non-repeating vibrations.

KEY_POINTS:

Noisy sounds are non-periodic and disorganized.
Sudden changes in vibration make sounds jarring.
Noise can cause discomfort, stress, or pain when very loud.

EXAMPLES:
Bus brake screeching, road jackhammer sound, and many people shouting together in a market are common noise examples.

KEYWORDS:
noise, irregular vibration, non-periodic wave, harsh sound, disturbance

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: C4
NAME: Waveforms and Oscilloscope Observation
DEFINITION:
An oscilloscope shows that music has a smooth repeating waveform, while noise has random jagged waveforms.

KEY_POINTS:

Musical sounds appear as periodic waves with repeating shapes.
Noise appears as non-periodic waves with irregular patterns.
The brain finds predictable patterns easier to process than random patterns.

EXAMPLES:
On an oscilloscope, a flute note shows repeating wave peaks, but traffic noise shows uneven, changing lines.

KEYWORDS:
oscilloscope, waveform, periodic, non-periodic, sound analysis

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: C5
NAME: Musical Instruments and Vibration Control
DEFINITION:
Musical instruments are designed to control vibration so that sound stays regular and pleasant.

KEY_POINTS:

Percussion instruments use stretched surfaces to produce controlled frequencies.
Wind instruments change the vibrating air column length to change notes.
String instruments use string thickness and tension to control pitch and sound quality.

EXAMPLES:
The Geta Beraya (Awanaddha), Horanewa or flute (Sushira), and violin or guitar (Taththa) each create music by controlling vibration in different ways.

KEYWORDS:
musical instruments, percussion, wind instruments, string instruments, frequency, Sri Lanka

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: C6
NAME: Subjectivity and Scientific Classification of Noise
DEFINITION:
Personal taste can differ, but in science, noise is identified mainly by disorganized vibration and harmful loudness.

KEY_POINTS:

One person may enjoy a sound that another person dislikes.
Scientific classification focuses on intensity and irregularity.
Very loud sounds that cause pain or stress are considered noise even if rhythmic.

EXAMPLES:
Loud heavy metal may be enjoyable to a fan but is still treated as noise if it disturbs sleep or causes stress.

KEYWORDS:
subjective sound, loudness, intensity, stress, scientific definition

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: C7
NAME: Noise Pollution and Health Effects
DEFINITION:
Noise pollution is continuous exposure to loud unpleasant sound, which can damage hearing and affect physical and mental health.

KEY_POINTS:

Constant noise can damage cochlear hair cells and cause permanent hearing loss.
Noise can increase blood pressure, headaches, insomnia, and stress.
Noisy study environments reduce concentration because the brain spends energy filtering disturbances.

EXAMPLES:
In busy areas of Colombo and Kandy, constant traffic and construction noise can make studying difficult and harm long-term hearing.

KEYWORDS:
noise pollution, cochlea, hearing loss, stress, insomnia, concentration, Colombo, Kandy

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: C8
NAME: Noise Control Methods and White Noise
DEFINITION:
Noise can be reduced by absorption, barriers, and source control, while white noise is a full-frequency sound sometimes used to mask sudden disturbances.

KEY_POINTS:

Soft materials absorb sound energy and reduce echo.
Walls and tree rows act as barriers that block or weaken sound.
Silencers and machine maintenance reduce noise at its source.
White noise contains many frequencies and can mask sudden irregular sounds.

EXAMPLES:
Planting trees along Sri Lankan roads reduces traffic sound, and a fan's white noise can help some people sleep by masking sudden barking sounds.

KEYWORDS:
absorption, barrier, source control, white noise, acoustic panel, silencer, noise buffer

SECTION: SUMMARY
Music and noise are both forms of sound made by vibrations, but they differ in vibration pattern and effect on listeners. Music usually comes from regular, repeating vibrations and appears as periodic waveforms, while noise comes from irregular vibrations and appears as non-periodic jagged waveforms. Musical instruments such as the Geta Beraya, Horanewa, flute, violin, and guitar are designed to control vibration so sounds remain pleasant and organized. Although sound preference can be personal, science classifies noise by disorganized pattern and harmful loudness. Continuous exposure to noise pollution in busy areas such as Colombo and Kandy can damage cochlear hair cells and cause stress, headaches, high blood pressure, insomnia, and reduced concentration in students. Noise can be controlled through absorption, barriers such as tree rows, and source control like silencers and proper machine maintenance. White noise, when used at safe levels, can mask sudden disturbing sounds for some people.
