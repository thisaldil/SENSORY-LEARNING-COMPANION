SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: SM2_01
NAME: Classification of Matter by Physical State
DEFINITION:
Matter can be grouped into three main physical states: solid, liquid, and gas, based on shape, volume, and particle arrangement.

KEY_POINTS:
- All matter is made of tiny particles.
- Physical state depends on particle behavior.
- Solids, liquids, and gases have different properties.
- Heat can change the state of matter.

KEYWORDS:
matter, physical state, solid, liquid, gas

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: SM2_02
NAME: Solids
DEFINITION:
Solids are materials that have a definite shape and definite volume because their particles are tightly packed.

KEY_POINTS:
- Solids keep their own shape.
- Solids occupy a fixed amount of space.
- Particles vibrate in fixed positions.
- Solids are difficult to compress.

EXAMPLES:
Firewood, bricks, stones, pens, jaggery.

KEYWORDS:
solid, definite shape, definite volume, rigid

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: SM2_03
NAME: Liquids
DEFINITION:
Liquids have a definite volume but no definite shape because their particles can slide past one another.

KEY_POINTS:
- Liquids take the shape of their container.
- Volume remains constant.
- Liquids can flow.
- Liquids are slightly compressible but difficult to squash.

EXAMPLES:
Kerosene, water, coconut oil, milk.

KEYWORDS:
liquid, flow, container shape, volume

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: SM2_04
NAME: Gases
DEFINITION:
Gases have neither definite shape nor definite volume because their particles move freely and spread out.

KEY_POINTS:
- Gases fill the entire container.
- Particles are far apart.
- Gases are easily compressed.
- Many gases are invisible.

EXAMPLES:
L.P. gas, air, oxygen, water vapour.

KEYWORDS:
gas, expansion, compression, particles far apart

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: SM2_05
NAME: Particle Theory of Matter
DEFINITION:
The particle theory explains that all matter is made of tiny particles whose arrangement and motion determine the state of matter.

KEY_POINTS:
- In solids, particles vibrate in fixed positions.
- In liquids, particles stay close but slide past each other.
- In gases, particles move rapidly and independently.
- Heat increases particle movement.

KEYWORDS:
particle theory, vibration, movement, temperature

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: SM2_06
NAME: Changes of State
DEFINITION:
Changes of state occur when heat is added or removed, causing matter to transform between solid, liquid, and gas.

KEY_POINTS:
- Melting: solid to liquid (absorbs heat).
- Freezing: liquid to solid (releases heat).
- Evaporation: liquid to gas (absorbs heat).
- Condensation: gas to liquid (releases heat).
- Heat energy changes particle motion.

EXAMPLES:
Ice melting into water.
Water vapour forming droplets on a cold bottle.
Rain forming from clouds.

KEYWORDS:
melting, freezing, evaporation, condensation

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: SM2_07
NAME: Measuring Mass and Volume
DEFINITION:
Mass and volume are measurable properties that help identify and compare different states of matter.

KEY_POINTS:
- Liquids are measured using a measuring cylinder.
- The curved surface of liquid is called the meniscus.
- Measurements must be read at eye level.
- Gases have mass even though they are light.

EXAMPLES:
Reading water level from bottom of meniscus.
Weighing a balloon before and after inflating it.

KEYWORDS:
mass, volume, measuring cylinder, meniscus, balance

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: SM2_08
NAME: Anomaly of Water
DEFINITION:
Water expands when it freezes, unlike most substances which contract when they become solid.

KEY_POINTS:
- Ice occupies more space than liquid water.
- Frozen water can crack containers.
- This property is unique and important in nature.

EXAMPLES:
Bottle cracking in freezer.
Ice floating on water.

KEYWORDS:
water expansion, freezing, anomaly, ice

==================================================
SECTION: RELATION
RELATION_ID: REL_SM2_01
FROM: Heat
TO: Change of State
EXPLANATION:
Adding heat increases particle motion, causing melting or evaporation, while removing heat causes freezing or condensation.

==================================================
SECTION: RELATION
RELATION_ID: REL_SM2_02
FROM: Particle Arrangement
TO: Physical Properties
EXPLANATION:
The spacing and movement of particles determine whether matter behaves as a solid, liquid, or gas.

SECTION: SUMMARY
Matter exists as solid, liquid, or gas depending on particle arrangement and motion. Solids have definite shape and volume, liquids have definite volume but no fixed shape, and gases have neither. Heat changes the motion of particles and causes phase transitions such as melting and evaporation. Understanding these concepts helps explain daily observations, laboratory measurements, and natural processes.
