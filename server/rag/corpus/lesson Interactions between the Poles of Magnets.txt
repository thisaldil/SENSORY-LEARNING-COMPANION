SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: C1
NAME: Magnetic Poles and Basic Interactions
DEFINITION:
Every magnet has two poles—a North Pole and a South Pole—and these poles exert forces on other poles depending on whether they are alike or different.

KEY_POINTS:

Magnets do not remain still near each other; they exert active forces.
Interactions between poles are predictable and follow consistent rules.
Two types of pole interactions exist: attraction and repulsion.

EXAMPLES:
A North Pole on one magnet will be pulled toward a South Pole on another magnet.

KEYWORDS:
magnetic pole, North Pole, South Pole, magnet interaction, force

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: C2
NAME: Law of Attraction
DEFINITION:
Unlike magnetic poles attract each other, exerting a pulling force that draws them together.

KEY_POINTS:

A North Pole and a South Pole move toward each other.
Opposite poles stick together when they touch.
Attraction is a pulling force.

EXAMPLES:
A North Pole and South Pole bar magnet placed near each other will move together and align.

KEYWORDS:
attraction, unlike poles, pulling force, opposite poles, magnet alignment

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: C3
NAME: Law of Repulsion
DEFINITION:
Like magnetic poles repel each other, exerting a pushing force that keeps them apart.

KEY_POINTS:

Two North Poles push away from each other.
Two South Poles push away from each other.
The repulsion feels like an invisible cushion of resistance.

EXAMPLES:
Two North Poles held close together create a pushing sensation that prevents them from touching easily.

KEYWORDS:
repulsion, like poles, pushing force, same poles, resistance

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: C4
NAME: Magnetic Field Lines
DEFINITION:
Magnetic field lines are invisible streams of magnetic energy that flow out of the North Pole and return into the South Pole.

KEY_POINTS:

Field lines show the pattern of magnetic force in space.
Lines from the North Pole can enter an approaching South Pole directly.
When two poles of the same type approach, their field lines push against each other.

EXAMPLES:
Using iron filings sprinkled around a magnet reveals field line patterns arcing from North to South.

KEYWORDS:
magnetic field, field lines, force pattern, North Pole flow, South Pole return

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: C5
NAME: Non-Contact Force
DEFINITION:
Magnetic force acts over a distance without physical contact between the poles, making magnetism a non-contact force.

KEY_POINTS:

Most forces require touching, but magnetic force works at a distance.
Closer poles produce stronger interactions.
Distance weakens the strength of both attraction and repulsion.

EXAMPLES:
A magnet can pull a paperclip across a table without touching it, or push another magnet without contact.

KEYWORDS:
non-contact force, distance force, action at distance, magnetic range

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: C6
NAME: Magnetic Induction
DEFINITION:
A magnetic pole can induce an opposite pole in a non-magnetic material such as iron, turning it into a temporary magnet.

KEY_POINTS:

A permanent magnet can make iron, steel, nickel, and cobalt become temporary magnets.
The induced pole is always opposite to the pole bringing on the induction.
Induced magnetism ends when the permanent magnet is removed.

EXAMPLES:
A North Pole brought near an iron nail induces a South Pole at the nail's tip, letting the nail be attracted.

KEYWORDS:
induction, induced pole, temporary magnet, iron, steel, nickel, cobalt

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: C7
NAME: Repulsion as the Definitive Magnet Test
DEFINITION:
Repulsion between two bars or objects is the only definite proof that both are permanent magnets, because attraction can also occur with induction.

KEY_POINTS:

Attraction alone does not prove both objects are magnets.
Repulsion proves permanent magnetism in both objects.
Scientists use "repulsion is the surest test for magnetism."

EXAMPLES:
If a bar pushes away another bar, both must be permanent magnets; if they only attract, the second bar might just be iron.

KEYWORDS:
magnet test, repulsion test, permanent magnet, proof, induction vs permanent

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: C8
NAME: Applications of Magnetic Pole Interactions
DEFINITION:
The attraction and repulsion between magnetic poles is used in practical devices and technologies.

KEY_POINTS:

Magnetic door catches use attraction to hold cupboard doors closed.
Maglev trains use repulsion to levitate and attraction to move forward.
Electric motors use alternating push and pull to create spinning motion.

EXAMPLES:
Many Sri Lankan home cupboards stay closed using magnetic catches that attract the door to the frame.

KEYWORDS:
magnetic applications, door catch, maglev train, electric motor, technology

Magnets attract when unlike poles face each other but repel when like poles face each other.

CORRECTION:
Iron can be attracted through induction without being a permanent magnet; only repulsion from another magnet proves permanent magnetism.

CORRECTION:
Magnetic force acts at a distance without contact, making it a non-contact force that decreases with distance.

SECTION: SUMMARY
Magnetic poles interact by exerting forces over a distance without touching. Unlike poles attract each other in a pulling motion, while like poles repel each other with a pushing force. These interactions are explained by magnetic field lines that flow from North Pole to South Pole; when unlike poles face each other, lines connect them like a stretched rubber band causing attraction, and when like poles face each other, lines clash causing repulsion. Magnetism is a non-contact force that decreases with distance. A permanent magnet can induce temporary magnetism in iron, steel, nickel, and cobalt, which explains how magnets pick up these materials. Repulsion is the definitive test for permanent magnetism because attraction alone can be caused by induction. Practical applications include magnetic door catches, Maglev trains that levitate using repulsion, and electric motors that spin from alternating attraction and repulsion, all of which are common technologies in and around Sri Lanka.
