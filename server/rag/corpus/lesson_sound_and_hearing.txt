SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: HL_01
NAME: Hearing
DEFINITION:
Hearing is the passive physiological process of detecting sound through the ears.

KEY_POINTS:
- Hearing happens automatically.
- It does not require attention.
- Sound waves enter the ear and stimulate the auditory system.
- A person can hear without understanding.

EXAMPLES:
Hearing traffic outside.
Hearing birds chirping.
Hearing background noise in a classroom.

KEYWORDS:
hearing, sound perception, ears, auditory system, passive process

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: HL_02
NAME: Listening
DEFINITION:
Listening is the active process of paying attention to sounds and understanding their meaning.

KEY_POINTS:
- Listening requires focus and attention.
- It involves understanding tone and context.
- It often includes responding appropriately.
- Listening builds communication skills.

EXAMPLES:
Paying attention to a teacher’s explanation.
Understanding a friend’s feelings during a conversation.
Following instructions carefully.

KEYWORDS:
listening, attention, understanding, communication, active skill

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: HL_03
NAME: Differences Between Hearing and Listening
DEFINITION:
Hearing is automatic sound detection, while listening is purposeful understanding and interpretation of sound.

KEY_POINTS:
- Hearing is passive; listening is active.
- Hearing detects sound; listening interprets meaning.
- Listening requires concentration.
- Effective communication depends on listening.

EXAMPLES:
A student hears classroom noise but listens to the teacher.
A person hears music but listens to the lyrics carefully.

KEYWORDS:
difference, passive, active, interpretation, meaning

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: HL_04
NAME: Importance of Active Listening
DEFINITION:
Active listening improves relationships, learning, and understanding by ensuring that messages are properly received and interpreted.

KEY_POINTS:
- Prevents misunderstandings.
- Builds trust and empathy.
- Improves academic performance.
- Encourages respectful communication.

EXAMPLES:
Maintaining eye contact during conversation.
Asking questions to clarify information.
Avoiding distractions while someone is speaking.

KEYWORDS:
active listening, empathy, communication skills, trust, focus

==================================================
SECTION: RELATION
RELATION_ID: REL_HL_01
FROM: Hearing
TO: Listening
EXPLANATION:
Listening builds upon hearing by adding attention, interpretation, and understanding to basic sound detection.

==================================================
SECTION: RELATION
RELATION_ID: REL_HL_02
FROM: Listening
TO: Effective Communication
EXPLANATION:
Effective communication requires active listening to ensure accurate understanding of messages.
