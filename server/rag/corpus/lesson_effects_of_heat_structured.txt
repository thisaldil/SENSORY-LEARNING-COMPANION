SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: CC_TEMPERATURE_CHANGE
NAME: Temperature Change
DEFINITION:
Temperature is a measure of how hot or cold a substance is. When heat energy is added, particles move faster and temperature increases; when heat is removed, particles move slower and temperature decreases.

KEY_POINTS:

- Heat naturally flows from hotter objects to cooler objects until temperatures become equal
- Thermometers are used to measure temperature
- Different materials warm and cool at different rates
- Temperature reflects particle motion at the microscopic level

EXAMPLES:
A metal spoon placed in hot tea becomes warm quickly because heat flows from the hotter tea to the cooler spoon.

KEYWORDS:
temperature, heat flow, hot to cold, thermometer, particle motion, heating rate

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: CC_CHANGE_OF_STATE
NAME: Change of State
DEFINITION:
Heat can change matter between solid, liquid, and gas by changing particle bonding and movement, while the substance itself can remain the same.

KEY_POINTS:

- Melting changes a solid to a liquid
- Freezing changes a liquid to a solid
- Vaporization (boiling) changes a liquid to a gas
- Condensation changes a gas to a liquid
- During a state change, added heat can be used to break bonds instead of raising temperature

EXAMPLES:
Ice cream melts on a hot Colombo afternoon, changing from solid to liquid because it absorbs heat from the surroundings.

KEYWORDS:
change of state, melting, freezing, vaporization, boiling, condensation, phase change, particle bonds

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: CC_THERMAL_EXPANSION
NAME: Thermal Expansion and Contraction
DEFINITION:
Most substances expand when heated because particles move faster and need more space, and contract when cooled because particles slow down and come closer together.

KEY_POINTS:

- Solids usually expand less than liquids
- Liquids generally expand less than gases
- Gases expand the most when heated
- Expansion and contraction are usually reversible for physical changes
- Engineers must design structures to safely handle expansion

EXAMPLES:
Railway tracks in Sri Lanka include small gaps so expanding steel rails do not buckle on very hot days.

KEYWORDS:
thermal expansion, contraction, particle spacing, solids liquids gases, expansion gaps, engineering design

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: CC_CHEMICAL_CHANGES_HEAT
NAME: Chemical Changes Caused by Heat
DEFINITION:
Strong heating can cause chemical reactions that form new substances with new properties, often making irreversible changes.

KEY_POINTS:

- Chemical changes can require threshold energy (activation energy)
- New substances with different color, taste, or texture can form
- Many heat-driven chemical changes are irreversible
- Burning is a chemical reaction where substances react with oxygen

EXAMPLES:
White sugar turns into brown caramel when heated, showing a chemical change that cannot be reversed to the original sugar crystals.

KEYWORDS:
chemical change, activation energy, irreversible change, caramelization, combustion, new substances

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: CC_HEAT_TRANSFER
NAME: Heat Energy Transfer
DEFINITION:
Heat moves from one place to another by conduction, convection, and radiation.

KEY_POINTS:

- Conduction is heat transfer through direct contact, common in solids
- Convection is heat transfer by movement of liquids or gases
- Radiation is heat transfer by electromagnetic waves and needs no medium
- Materials differ in conductivity, affecting how fast they transfer heat

EXAMPLES:
A metal pot handle gets hot by conduction while warm air rising in a kitchen is convection, and sunlight warming your skin is radiation.

KEYWORDS:
heat transfer, conduction, convection, radiation, conductivity, thermal energy flow

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: CC_STATE_AND_EXPANSION_RELATION
NAME: State Change and Expansion Behavior
DEFINITION:
State changes and expansion are related because particle arrangement and spacing change with heat, and volume may change significantly across states.

KEY_POINTS:

- Heating from solid to liquid to gas usually increases particle spacing and volume
- Cooling usually reduces spacing and volume
- Water is unusual because it expands when freezing
- This special behavior of water affects natural and engineering processes

EXAMPLES:
When water freezes in a closed space, its expansion can crack containers because ice occupies more volume than liquid water.

KEYWORDS:
volume change, state and expansion, particle arrangement, water anomaly, freezing expansion

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: CC_HEAT_SAFETY
NAME: Heat Safety in Daily Life
DEFINITION:
Understanding heat effects is essential for safety because thermal expansion, high temperatures, and pressure build-up can cause accidents.

KEY_POINTS:

- Closed containers should not be left in strong sunlight because heated gases can expand and increase pressure
- Very hot materials can cause burns and must be handled carefully
- Engineering safety features like expansion joints and rail gaps prevent structural failure
- Safe cooking and heating practices reduce fire and burn risks

EXAMPLES:
A tightly sealed soft-drink bottle left in hot sunlight can become dangerous because gas expansion increases internal pressure.

KEYWORDS:
heat safety, pressure build-up, burns, expansion joints, thermal risk, safe handling

RELATION_ID: REL_TEMP_STATE

TO: Change of State
EXPLANATION:
Rising temperature provides energy that can bring substances to melting or boiling points, enabling state changes. Temperature change is therefore a key condition for phase transitions.

SECTION: RELATION
RELATION_ID: REL_TEMP_EXPANSION
FROM: Temperature Change
TO: Thermal Expansion and Contraction
EXPLANATION:
As temperature increases, particle motion increases and materials expand. As temperature decreases, particle motion decreases and materials contract.

==================================================
SECTION: RELATION
RELATION_ID: REL_TRANSFER_TEMP
FROM: Heat Energy Transfer
TO: Temperature Change
EXPLANATION:
Conduction, convection, and radiation are the mechanisms that move heat into or out of substances. This movement directly causes temperature to rise or fall.

==================================================
SECTION: RELATION
RELATION_ID: REL_CHEMICAL_THRESHOLD
FROM: Change of State
TO: Chemical Changes Caused by Heat
EXPLANATION:
State changes are usually physical changes where the substance can remain chemically the same, while chemical changes require enough energy to break and form chemical bonds, creating new substances.

==================================================

TO: Heat Safety in Daily Life

SECTION: EXAMPLE
EXAMPLE_ID: EX_MORNING_TEA
TITLE: Morning Tea Cooling to Room Temperature

DESCRIPTION:
Freshly made tea starts hot because its particles have high thermal energy. Heat transfers from the tea to the cup and then to the cooler air by conduction and convection. Over time, the tea temperature drops until it approaches room temperature, showing that heat naturally flows from hot to cold.

==================================================
SECTION: EXAMPLE
EXAMPLE_ID: EX_ICE_DIFFERENT_SURFACES
TITLE: Ice Cubes Melting on Different Materials

DESCRIPTION:
Ice cubes placed on metal, wood, and plastic melt at different rates. The cube on metal melts fastest because metal is a good conductor, while wood slows heat transfer and melts ice more slowly. All cubes eventually melt, showing that heat causes state change while material type controls the speed.

==================================================
SECTION: EXAMPLE
EXAMPLE_ID: EX_RAILWAY_GAPS
TITLE: Railway Track Gaps in Sri Lanka

DESCRIPTION:
Railway tracks near areas such as Colombo Fort and Anuradhapura include expansion gaps between steel sections. On hot days, the rails expand and gaps become smaller. Without these gaps, rails could buckle upward and create derailment risk.

==================================================
SECTION: EXAMPLE
EXAMPLE_ID: EX_HOPPERS_COOKING
TITLE: Heat Effects While Making Hoppers

DESCRIPTION:
When batter is poured into a very hot hopper pan, heat quickly raises temperature, evaporates water, and drives chemical reactions that create crispy edges and a soft center. These texture and flavor changes are not fully reversible, demonstrating heat-driven chemical change during cooking.

==================================================
SECTION: EXAMPLE
EXAMPLE_ID: EX_BALLOON_SUNLIGHT
TITLE: Balloon Expansion in Sunlight

DESCRIPTION:
A partially inflated balloon left in sunlight becomes tighter and larger because the air inside warms and expands. When moved to a cooler air-conditioned room, the gas cools and contracts, and the balloon shrinks slightly.

==================================================
SECTION: MISCONCEPTION
MISCONCEPTION_ID: MISC_HEAT_TEMP_SAME
STATEMENT:
Heat and temperature are exactly the same thing.

CORRECTION:
Heat is energy transferred between objects due to temperature difference, while temperature measures how hot or cold something is. Heat transfer can change temperature, but the terms are not identical.

==================================================
SECTION: MISCONCEPTION
MISCONCEPTION_ID: MISC_STATE_CHANGE_ALWAYS_TEMP_RISES
STATEMENT:
Temperature always rises while heating, even during boiling or melting.

CORRECTION:
During melting or boiling, added heat can be used to break particle bonds rather than increase temperature. That is why temperature can remain constant during a phase change.

==================================================
SECTION: MISCONCEPTION
MISCONCEPTION_ID: MISC_EXPANSION_ONLY_METALS
STATEMENT:
Only metals expand when heated.

CORRECTION:
Most solids, liquids, and gases expand when heated, though by different amounts. Gases generally expand the most, liquids more than most solids.

==================================================
SECTION: MISCONCEPTION
MISCONCEPTION_ID: MISC_CHEMICAL_CHANGES_REVERSIBLE
STATEMENT:
All heat changes can be reversed by cooling.

CORRECTION:
Some physical changes like wax melting can reverse on cooling, but many heat-driven chemical changes (such as caramelization or burning) form new substances and are not reversible.

==================================================
SECTION: MISCONCEPTION
MISCONCEPTION_ID: MISC_SEALED_BOTTLE_SAFE_IN_SUN
STATEMENT:
A sealed bottle is always safe to leave in direct hot sunlight.

CORRECTION:
Heating can expand gases and increase pressure inside closed containers. This can make containers deform or burst, so sealed bottles should not be left in strong heat.

SECTION: SUMMARY
Heat is a form of energy that changes the world by changing temperature, state, size, and sometimes chemical composition. Heat flows naturally from hotter objects to cooler ones and can be transferred by conduction, convection, and radiation. With enough heat, matter changes state between solid, liquid, and gas, and during phase changes temperature may stay constant while energy breaks particle bonds. Most substances expand when heated and contract when cooled, which is why engineers in Sri Lanka use rail gaps and expansion joints for safety. Strong heating can also cause chemical reactions, such as caramelization and burning, that form new substances and are often irreversible. Domain-specific applications in cooking, transport, medicine, and home life show that understanding heat is essential for science, engineering, and daily safety.
