==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: HE_01
NAME: Heat as an Environmental Driver
DEFINITION:
Heat from the Sun controls environmental temperature and supports life on Earth.

KEY_POINTS:
- The Sun heats the Earth's surface during the day.
- The Earth releases heat at night.
- This balance maintains suitable living conditions.
- Too much trapped heat leads to environmental imbalance.

EXAMPLES:
Daytime warming and nighttime cooling cycle.

KEYWORDS:
solar heat, temperature balance, environmental heat

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: HE_02
NAME: Global Warming
DEFINITION:
Global warming is the gradual increase in Earth’s average temperature due to excessive heat being trapped in the atmosphere.

KEY_POINTS:
- Caused by increased greenhouse gases.
- Leads to rising temperatures worldwide.
- Contributes to melting glaciers and rising sea levels.
- Affects climate stability.

EXAMPLES:
Melting polar ice.
Rising sea levels affecting island nations.

KEYWORDS:
global warming, greenhouse gases, climate change, sea level rise

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: HE_03
NAME: Greenhouse Effect
DEFINITION:
The greenhouse effect is the natural process by which certain gases in the atmosphere trap heat and keep Earth warm.

KEY_POINTS:
- Gases such as carbon dioxide and methane trap heat.
- Essential for maintaining life-supporting temperatures.
- Excess greenhouse gases cause overheating.
- Human activities increase greenhouse gas levels.

EXAMPLES:
Burning coal, oil, and deforestation increase carbon dioxide.

KEYWORDS:
greenhouse effect, carbon dioxide, methane, atmosphere

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: HE_04
NAME: Effects on Living Organisms
DEFINITION:
Environmental heat influences how living organisms survive and function.

KEY_POINTS:
- Homeothermic animals regulate body temperature internally.
- Poikilothermic animals depend on environmental temperature.
- Extreme heat stresses ecosystems.
- Species may migrate to cooler areas.

EXAMPLES:
Lizards hiding in shade.
Birds moving to cooler hill regions.

KEYWORDS:
homeothermic, poikilothermic, adaptation, migration

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: HE_05
NAME: Heat and the Water Cycle
DEFINITION:
Heat drives the evaporation process and powers the water cycle.

KEY_POINTS:
- Heat causes evaporation from oceans and rivers.
- Warm air rises and forms clouds.
- Excess heat can intensify droughts.
- Warm oceans increase storm intensity.

EXAMPLES:
Droughts in Hambantota.
Strong cyclones forming over warm ocean water.

KEYWORDS:
evaporation, drought, cyclones, water cycle

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: HE_06
NAME: Thermal Expansion of Oceans
DEFINITION:
When ocean water warms, it expands, contributing to sea-level rise.

KEY_POINTS:
- Heat increases molecular movement.
- Warm water occupies more space.
- Contributes significantly to rising sea levels.
- Combined with melting ice, increases flooding risk.

EXAMPLES:
Coastal flooding risks in island regions.

KEYWORDS:
thermal expansion, sea-level rise, ocean warming

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: HE_07
NAME: Urban Heat Island Effect
DEFINITION:
The Urban Heat Island effect occurs when cities become warmer than surrounding rural areas due to heat-absorbing surfaces.

KEY_POINTS:
- Concrete and asphalt absorb heat.
- Reduced tree cover increases warming.
- Cities release stored heat slowly at night.
- Planting trees reduces temperature.

EXAMPLES:
Colombo feeling hotter than rural villages.

KEYWORDS:
urban heat island, city warming, asphalt, tree cover

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: HE_08
NAME: Heat and Ecological Timing
DEFINITION:
Temperature influences seasonal behaviors such as flowering, nesting, and migration.

KEY_POINTS:
- Plants bloom based on temperature signals.
- Birds nest when conditions are suitable.
- Early warming can disrupt food availability.
- Extreme changes threaten biodiversity.

EXAMPLES:
Tabebuia trees blooming during hot seasons.
Bird nesting patterns changing.

KEYWORDS:
seasonal timing, ecological balance, biodiversity

==================================================

Excess greenhouse gases strengthen the natural greenhouse effect, leading to global warming.

SECTION: MISCONCEPTION
MISCONCEPTION_ID: MC_HE_01
STATEMENT:
The greenhouse effect is entirely harmful.

CORRECTION:
The natural greenhouse effect is essential for life; problems arise when excess gases increase heat trapping.

==================================================
SECTION: MISCONCEPTION
MISCONCEPTION_ID: MC_HE_02
STATEMENT:
Only melting ice causes sea-level rise.

CORRECTION:
Sea levels rise due to both melting ice and thermal expansion of warm ocean water.

==================================================
SECTION: MISCONCEPTION
MISCONCEPTION_ID: MC_HE_03
STATEMENT:
Temperature changes do not affect animals.

CORRECTION:
Temperature strongly affects animal survival, migration, and reproduction patterns.

SECTION: SUMMARY
Heat from the Sun controls Earth’s climate and supports life. However, excessive heat trapped by greenhouse gases causes global warming, rising sea levels, and ecosystem disruption. Heat influences animal survival, weather patterns, and ocean behavior. Human actions such as planting trees, conserving energy, and reducing waste can help manage environmental heat.
