SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: FM_01
NAME: Force
DEFINITION:
A force is a push or a pull that can change the motion or shape of an object.

KEY_POINTS:
- A force can start motion.
- A force can stop motion.
- A force can change speed.
- A force can change direction.
- Force is measured in newtons (N).

EXAMPLES:
Pushing a door
Pulling a rope
Kicking a ball

KEYWORDS:
push, pull, change motion, newton

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: FM_02
NAME: Motion
DEFINITION:
Motion is the change in position of an object over time.

KEY_POINTS:
- An object is in motion when it changes position.
- Motion depends on time and distance.
- Objects can move fast or slow.

EXAMPLES:
A car moving on the road
A bird flying
A rolling ball

KEYWORDS:
movement, position, time, distance

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: FM_03
NAME: Gravity
DEFINITION:
Gravity is a force that pulls objects toward the Earth.

KEY_POINTS:
- Gravity keeps us on the ground.
- It causes objects to fall.
- The Earth pulls everything toward its center.

EXAMPLES:
An apple falling from a tree
Rain falling to the ground

KEYWORDS:
pull, Earth, falling

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: FM_04
NAME: Friction
DEFINITION:
Friction is a force that resists motion when two surfaces rub against each other.

KEY_POINTS:
- Friction slows objects down.
- It allows us to walk without slipping.
- Rough surfaces create more friction.

EXAMPLES:
Brakes stopping a bicycle
Shoes gripping the ground

KEYWORDS:
resistance, rubbing, slow down

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: FM_05
NAME: Newton’s First Law
DEFINITION:
An object will remain at rest or continue moving in a straight line unless acted upon by a force.

KEY_POINTS:
- Also called the law of inertia.
- Objects resist changes in motion.

EXAMPLE:
A book stays on a table until someone pushes it.

KEYWORDS:
inertia, rest, constant motion

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: FM_06
NAME: Newton’s Second Law
DEFINITION:
Force equals mass times acceleration.

FORMULA:
F = m × a

KEY_POINTS:
- Heavier objects need more force to move.
- More force causes greater acceleration.

EXAMPLE:
It is harder to push a truck than a bicycle.

KEYWORDS:
mass, acceleration, formula

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: FM_07
NAME: Newton’s Third Law
DEFINITION:
For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.

KEY_POINTS:
- Forces come in pairs.
- When one object pushes another, the second pushes back.

EXAMPLE:
A rocket moves forward when gases push backward.

KEYWORDS:
action, reaction, opposite force

TITLE: Apple Falling from a Tree

DESCRIPTION:
Gravity pulls the apple downward.

==================================================
SECTION: EXAMPLE
EXAMPLE_ID: EX_FM_02
TITLE: Riding a Bicycle

DESCRIPTION:
You push the pedals.
The bicycle moves forward.
Friction between tires and road allows movement.
Brakes create friction to stop the bicycle.

==================================================
SECTION: MISCONCEPTION
MISCONCEPTION_ID: MC_FM_01
STATEMENT:
If an object is moving, a force must always be pushing it.

CORRECTION:
An object can continue moving without a force unless friction or another force acts on it.
