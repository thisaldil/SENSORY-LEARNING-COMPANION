SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: WA_01
NAME: Surface Water
DEFINITION:
Surface water is water found on the surface of the Earth and is easily visible.

KEY_POINTS:
- Found in rivers, lakes, ponds, and seas.
- Directly affected by rainfall.
- Used for agriculture, drinking, and industry.
- Easily polluted by human activities.

EXAMPLES:
Streams, rivers, tanks (Wewa), lakes, sea.
Ancient irrigation tanks in Sri Lanka.

KEYWORDS:
surface water, rivers, lakes, tanks, agriculture

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: WA_02
NAME: Ground Water
DEFINITION:
Ground water is water that seeps into the soil and collects in underground layers of rock and sand.

KEY_POINTS:
- Formed by rainwater infiltration.
- Stored in pores and cracks in rocks.
- Accessed using wells and tube wells.
- Naturally filtered but may contain dissolved minerals.

EXAMPLES:
Well water.
Tube well water.
Ground water in Jaffna limestone regions.

KEYWORDS:
ground water, infiltration, wells, underground water

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: WA_03
NAME: Atmospheric Water
DEFINITION:
Atmospheric water is water present in the air as vapour or tiny droplets.

KEY_POINTS:
- Exists as invisible water vapour.
- Forms clouds through condensation.
- Appears as dew, mist, and rain.
- Drives weather systems.

EXAMPLES:
Clouds, mist in Nuwara Eliya, dew on grass.

KEYWORDS:
atmospheric water, vapour, clouds, condensation

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: WA_04
NAME: Aquifers
DEFINITION:
An aquifer is an underground layer of rock or soil that stores significant amounts of ground water.

KEY_POINTS:
- Water fills tiny pores and cracks.
- Important for drinking water supply.
- Vulnerable to pollution.
- Limestone aquifers are important in northern Sri Lanka.

EXAMPLES:
Limestone aquifers in Jaffna.

KEYWORDS:
aquifer, underground storage, limestone, groundwater supply

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: WA_05
NAME: Types of Surface Water
DEFINITION:
Surface water can be classified based on salt content as freshwater, marine water, or brackish water.

KEY_POINTS:
- Freshwater contains very little salt.
- Marine water contains high salt content.
- Brackish water is a mixture of fresh and salt water.
- Lagoons often contain brackish water.

EXAMPLES:
Freshwater rivers and tanks.
Indian Ocean (marine water).
Negombo Lagoon (brackish water).

KEYWORDS:
freshwater, marine water, brackish water, lagoon

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: WA_06
NAME: Water Table
DEFINITION:
The water table is the upper level of underground water where soil and rocks are fully saturated.

KEY_POINTS:
- Rises during rainy seasons.
- Falls during dry seasons.
- Affected by over-extraction.
- Important for agriculture and wells.

EXAMPLES:
Wells drying during drought.
Higher well water levels after monsoon.

KEYWORDS:
water table, saturation, rainy season, drought

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: WA_07
NAME: Water Quality and Human Impact
DEFINITION:
Water quality can change due to natural processes and human activities such as pollution and overuse.

KEY_POINTS:
- Rainwater may collect air pollutants.
- Surface water can be polluted by waste.
- Ground water can absorb chemicals from fertilizers.
- Protecting water sources is essential for health.

EXAMPLES:
Plastic waste in rivers.
Fertilizer runoff affecting wells.
Groundwater quality studies in North Central Province.

KEYWORDS:
water pollution, fertilizer runoff, contamination, water protection

==================================================
SECTION: RELATION
RELATION_ID: REL_WA_01
FROM: Evaporation
TO: Atmospheric Water
EXPLANATION:
Evaporation converts surface water into atmospheric water vapour, which later forms clouds and rain.

==================================================
SECTION: RELATION
RELATION_ID: REL_WA_02
FROM: Rainfall
TO: Ground Water
EXPLANATION:
Rainwater infiltrates the soil and replenishes underground aquifers.

==================================================
SECTION: RELATION
RELATION_ID: REL_WA_03
FROM: Over-Extraction
TO: Water Table Decline
EXPLANATION:
Pumping too much groundwater lowers the water table and may cause wells to dry.

==================================================
SECTION: EXAMPLE
EXAMPLE_ID: EX_WA_01

Ancient Sri Lankan kings built large tanks (Wewa) to store surface water for farming.

==================================================
SECTION: EXAMPLE
EXAMPLE_ID: EX_WA_02
TITLE: Negombo Lagoon

DESCRIPTION:
Negombo Lagoon contains brackish water where river water mixes with seawater.

==================================================
SECTION: EXAMPLE
EXAMPLE_ID: EX_WA_03
TITLE: Rising and Falling Water Table

DESCRIPTION:
During heavy rain, wells fill easily. During drought, wells may dry as the water table drops.

==================================================
SECTION: MISCONCEPTION
MISCONCEPTION_ID: MC_WA_01
STATEMENT:
Ground water exists in large underground rivers and empty caves.

CORRECTION:
Ground water mainly fills tiny pores and cracks in rocks and soil layers.

==================================================
SECTION: MISCONCEPTION
MISCONCEPTION_ID: MC_WA_02
STATEMENT:
Rainwater is always completely pure.

CORRECTION:
Rainwater can absorb pollutants from the air before reaching the ground.

==================================================
SECTION: MISCONCEPTION
MISCONCEPTION_ID: MC_WA_03
STATEMENT:
Sea water can be used directly for drinking and farming.

CORRECTION:
Marine water contains high salt content and requires treatment before use.

SECTION: LEARNING_OBJECTIVES
- Identify surface water, ground water, and atmospheric water.
- Explain how aquifers store underground water.
- Distinguish freshwater, marine water, and brackish water.
- Describe the importance of the water table.
- Explain how human activities affect water quality.

==================================================
SECTION: SUMMARY
Water exists as surface water, ground water, and atmospheric water depending on location and availability. Ground water is stored in aquifers and influenced by the water table. Surface water may be freshwater, marine, or brackish. Atmospheric water drives weather through evaporation and condensation. Human activities can affect water quality, making conservation and protection essential.
