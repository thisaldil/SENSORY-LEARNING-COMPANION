SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: C1
NAME: Weather as Short-Term Atmospheric Condition
DEFINITION:
Weather is the condition of the atmosphere at a specific place during a short period of time.

KEY_POINTS:

Weather describes current atmospheric state.
The atmosphere is always moving, so weather changes quickly.
Conditions can vary greatly within a single day.

EXAMPLES:
A morning may be sunny in a village but become rainy by afternoon due to rapid atmospheric changes.

KEYWORDS:
weather, atmosphere, short-term, atmospheric conditions, rapid change

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: C2
NAME: Elements of Weather
DEFINITION:
Weather elements are measurable characteristics of the atmosphere including temperature, humidity, wind, precipitation, and cloud cover.

KEY_POINTS:

Temperature measures how hot or cold the air is.
Humidity measures water vapor content in air.
Wind is measured by speed and direction.
Precipitation includes rain, dew, and hail.
Cloud cover measures how much sky is hidden by clouds.

EXAMPLES:
A weather report might state 28°C temperature, high humidity, 15 km/h southeast wind, and scattered clouds.

KEYWORDS:
temperature, humidity, wind, precipitation, cloud cover, weather elements

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: C3
NAME: Weather Measuring Instruments
DEFINITION:
Specialized instruments measure different weather elements accurately for scientific study and forecasting.

KEY_POINTS:

Thermometer measures air temperature.
Rain gauge measures rainfall in millimeters.
Anemometer measures wind speed.
Wind vane shows wind direction.

EXAMPLES:
A school weather station uses a rain gauge to record 45 mm of rainfall during a storm.

KEYWORDS:
thermometer, rain gauge, anemometer, wind vane, weather instruments, measurement

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: C4
NAME: Air Pressure and Weather Patterns
DEFINITION:
Air pressure is the weight of air pressing down, and its changes create different weather patterns.

KEY_POINTS:

Warm air rises and creates low pressure, bringing clouds and rain.
Cool air sinks and creates high pressure, bringing clear skies.
Pressure changes over the Indian Ocean can form depressions or cyclones affecting Sri Lanka.

EXAMPLES:
Sudden air pressure drops over the Indian Ocean can lead to cyclones bringing heavy rain and wind to Sri Lanka.

KEYWORDS:
air pressure, low pressure, high pressure, cyclone, depression, Indian Ocean

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: C5
NAME: Cloud Formation Through Condensation
DEFINITION:
Clouds form when rising warm air cools, causing water vapor to condense into tiny droplets that gather together.

KEY_POINTS:

Sun heats ground, warming air above it.
Warm air rises and cools at higher altitude.
Water vapor condenses into tiny droplets forming clouds.
Heavy droplets fall as rain when air cannot hold them.

EXAMPLES:
On a hot day, air rises from heated ground and forms afternoon clouds that may produce rain.

KEYWORDS:
cloud formation, condensation, water vapor, rising air, evaporation, precipitation

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: C6
NAME: Wind Formation and Monsoons
DEFINITION:
Wind is caused by uneven Earth heating, and monsoons are seasonal wind patterns bringing rain to Sri Lanka.

KEY_POINTS:

Equatorial regions receive more direct sunlight than poles.
Warm air rises at equator while cool air from poles moves in, creating wind.
South-West Monsoon brings rain May to September.
North-East Monsoon brings rain December to February.
Monsoon water supports electricity generation and rice farming.

EXAMPLES:
The South-West Monsoon provides essential water for Sri Lankan rice paddies and hydroelectric dams.

KEYWORDS:
wind, uneven heating, monsoon, South-West Monsoon, North-East Monsoon, seasonal wind, rice fields

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: C7
NAME: Weather versus Climate
DEFINITION:
Weather is the current or short-term atmospheric condition, while climate is the average weather over a long period, typically 30 years.

KEY_POINTS:

Weather describes today or right now.
Climate describes long-term average patterns.
Weather changes daily; climate describes typical conditions.

EXAMPLES:
Kandy may have rain today (weather), but Nuwara Eliya has a cool climate (long-term average).

KEYWORDS:
weather, climate, short-term, long-term average, Kandy, Nuwara Eliya

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: C8
NAME: Humidity and Human Comfort
DEFINITION:
Humidity is water vapor concentration in air, and high humidity reduces evaporative cooling, making people feel hotter.

KEY_POINTS:

High humidity slows sweat evaporation.
Evaporation is how the body cools itself.
Same temperature feels hotter in humid areas than dry areas.

EXAMPLES:
A 30°C day in dry Vavuniya feels more comfortable than the same temperature in humid Colombo.

KEYWORDS:
humidity, water vapor, evaporation, body cooling, comfort, Vavuniya, Colombo

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: C9
NAME: Weather Forecasting and Meteorology
DEFINITION:
Weather forecasting uses observations from instruments, satellites, and radar to predict future atmospheric conditions.

KEY_POINTS:

Meteorologists are scientists who study and predict weather.
Department of Meteorology uses satellites, radar, and weather stations.
Forecasts warn fishermen about dangerous seas.
Forecasts help farmers plan harvest timing.

EXAMPLES:
The Department of Meteorology issues storm warnings to prevent fishermen from going to sea during dangerous conditions.

KEYWORDS:
weather forecasting, meteorology, satellite, radar, Department of Meteorology, fishermen warnings, farmer planning

SECTION: SUMMARY
Weather is the short-term atmospheric condition at a specific place, changing rapidly as the atmosphere moves. Scientists describe weather using elements such as temperature, humidity, wind, precipitation, and cloud cover, measured by instruments including thermometers, rain gauges, anemometers, and wind vanes. Air pressure plays a key role, with low pressure bringing clouds and rain while high pressure brings clear skies, and pressure changes over the Indian Ocean can create cyclones affecting Sri Lanka. Clouds form when rising warm air cools and water vapor condenses into tiny droplets. Wind results from uneven Earth heating, and Sri Lanka experiences important seasonal monsoons—the South-West Monsoon from May to September and North-East Monsoon from December to February—that provide water for electricity and rice farming. Weather differs from climate as short-term conditions versus long-term averages over 30 years. High humidity reduces evaporative cooling and makes people feel hotter, explaining why temperatures feel different in Vavuniya versus Colombo. Meteorologists use satellites, radar, and weather stations to forecast conditions, helping save lives through warnings to fishermen and supporting farmers in harvest planning.
