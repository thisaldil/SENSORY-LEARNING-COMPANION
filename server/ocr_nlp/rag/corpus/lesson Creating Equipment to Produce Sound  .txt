SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: SE_01
NAME: Sound and Vibration
DEFINITION:
Sound is produced when an object vibrates and causes the surrounding air to vibrate.

KEY_POINTS:
- Vibrations move back and forth rapidly.
- Vibrations travel through air as sound waves.
- Without vibration, no sound is produced.

EXAMPLES:
Plucking a rubber band.
Striking a drum.
Blowing into a whistle.

KEYWORDS:
vibration, sound waves, movement

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: SE_02
NAME: Tension and Pitch
DEFINITION:
The tightness of a vibrating object affects the pitch of the sound.

KEY_POINTS:
- Higher tension produces higher pitch.
- Lower tension produces lower pitch.
- Tight rubber bands vibrate faster.

EXAMPLES:
Tightening a rubber band on a box.
Pulling a drum skin tighter.

KEYWORDS:
tension, pitch, frequency

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: SE_03
NAME: Length and Pitch
DEFINITION:
The length of a vibrating object or air column affects the pitch.

KEY_POINTS:
- Longer length produces lower pitch.
- Shorter length produces higher pitch.
- Air inside flutes vibrates to produce sound.

EXAMPLES:
Long bamboo flute makes a low note.
Short whistle makes a high note.

KEYWORDS:
air column, length, frequency

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: SE_04
NAME: Resonance
DEFINITION:
Resonance occurs when vibrations cause surrounding air or objects to vibrate at the same frequency, increasing sound volume.

KEY_POINTS:
- Hollow bodies amplify sound.
- Sound becomes louder when resonance occurs.
- Musical instruments use resonance chambers.

EXAMPLES:
Rubber band over a box is louder.
Guitars and violins have hollow bodies.
Rabana drum produces loud sound.

KEYWORDS:
amplification, resonance chamber, vibration

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: SE_05
NAME: Mass and Inertia in Sound
DEFINITION:
The mass of a vibrating object affects how quickly it can vibrate.

KEY_POINTS:
- More mass vibrates slowly.
- Less mass vibrates quickly.
- Faster vibration produces higher pitch.

EXAMPLES:
Bottle with more water produces low sound.
Bottle with less water produces high sound.

KEYWORDS:
mass, inertia, pitch

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: SE_06
NAME: Loudspeaker Technology
DEFINITION:
A loudspeaker produces sound by moving a cone back and forth using an electromagnet.

KEY_POINTS:
- Electricity controls the magnet.
- The cone vibrates rapidly.
- Vibrations create complex sounds.

EXAMPLES:
Television speakers.
Smartphone speakers.

KEYWORDS:
electromagnet, cone, electronic sound

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: SE_07
NAME: Environmental Responsibility in Sound Creation
DEFINITION:
Sound-producing devices can be made using recycled or natural materials to protect the environment.

KEY_POINTS:
- Use discarded materials.
- Practice upcycling.
- Reduce environmental waste.

EXAMPLES:
Using plastic bottles as instruments.
Using fallen coconut leaves.

KEYWORDS:
upcycling, recycling, sustainability

TITLE: Tin Can Drum
