SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: C1
NAME: North-South Alignment of a Freely Suspended Magnet
DEFINITION:
A freely suspended bar magnet always aligns in the North-South direction because Earth acts like a giant magnet.

KEY_POINTS:

The end pointing north is called the North Pole.
The end pointing south is called the South Pole.
This predictable alignment is the principle used in compasses for navigation.

EXAMPLES:
When a bar magnet is tied with thread and allowed to swing freely, it settles in the North-South direction.

KEYWORDS:
freely suspended magnet, North-South alignment, North Pole, South Pole, compass, Earth magnetism

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: C2
NAME: Law of Magnetism and Non-Contact Force
DEFINITION:
Magnets follow the law that like poles repel and unlike poles attract through an invisible magnetic field, without direct contact.

KEY_POINTS:

North-North and South-South interactions cause repulsion.
North-South interactions cause attraction.
Magnetic force acts at a distance through the magnetic field.

EXAMPLES:
Two bar magnets push apart when like poles face each other and pull together when opposite poles face each other.

KEYWORDS:
law of magnetism, attraction, repulsion, magnetic field, non-contact force

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: C3
NAME: Magnetic Induction in Iron and Steel
DEFINITION:
Magnetic induction is the temporary magnetization of materials like iron or steel when placed near a magnet.

KEY_POINTS:

A strong magnet can induce magnetism in nearby iron objects.
Induced objects can attract other small magnetic items.
Induced magnetism usually disappears when the main magnet is removed.

EXAMPLES:
An iron nail touched by a magnet can pick up paperclips for a short time.

KEYWORDS:
magnetic induction, temporary magnet, iron nail, steel, induced magnetism

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: C4
NAME: Earth’s Magnetic Core and Biological Navigation
DEFINITION:
Earth’s magnetic field is generated by moving liquid iron in the core, and some animals use this field to navigate.

KEY_POINTS:

Earth’s core motion creates large-scale magnetic field lines.
Freely suspended magnets align with these field lines, clearly observable in Sri Lanka near the equator.
Birds, sea turtles, and bees may use magnetic particles in their bodies for migration.

EXAMPLES:
Migratory birds can travel long distances while maintaining direction using Earth’s magnetic cues.

KEYWORDS:
Earth core, geomagnetic field, equatorial alignment, animal navigation, migration

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: C5
NAME: Magnetic Force Through Materials and Shielding
DEFINITION:
Magnetic force can pass through many non-magnetic materials but is weakened or redirected by magnetic metals such as iron and steel.

KEY_POINTS:

Magnetic force passes through wood, plastic, glass, and water.
Iron or steel barriers absorb and redirect magnetic field lines.
Metal enclosures can reduce magnetic interference for sensitive equipment.

EXAMPLES:
A magnet can move pins through a wooden table, but an iron sheet between them blocks the effect.

KEYWORDS:
magnetic shielding, non-magnetic materials, iron barrier, steel barrier, magnetic interference

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: C6
NAME: Breaking a Magnet and Pole Pairing
DEFINITION:
Breaking a magnet creates smaller complete magnets, each with both North and South poles.

KEY_POINTS:

Isolated single poles are not produced by breaking magnets.
Each fragment remains a dipole with two poles.
Atomic-level magnet alignment remains within each broken piece.

EXAMPLES:
When a bar magnet is snapped into two pieces, both pieces act as smaller magnets with their own North and South poles.

KEYWORDS:
magnet breaking, dipole, pole pairing, atomic magnets, no monopole

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: C7
NAME: Demagnetization by Heat and Physical Shock
DEFINITION:
Demagnetization is the loss of magnetic behavior when atomic alignment is disturbed by heating or repeated impacts.

KEY_POINTS:

High temperature causes strong atomic vibration and disorder.
Hammering or dropping can disrupt internal magnetic alignment.
Careful handling helps magnets keep their strength longer.

EXAMPLES:
A repeatedly dropped magnet gradually loses its ability to attract iron filings strongly.

KEYWORDS:
demagnetization, heat effect, physical shock, atomic alignment, magnet care

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: C8
NAME: Effect of Magnet Shape on Force Distribution
DEFINITION:
Magnet shape changes how magnetic field strength is distributed, affecting lifting power and applications.

KEY_POINTS:

Bar magnets spread force over a wider region.
Horseshoe magnets concentrate field between close opposite poles.
Concentrated fields allow stronger lifting for industrial and transport uses.

EXAMPLES:
A horseshoe magnet of similar size can lift more metal than a bar magnet because its poles are closer and field concentration is higher.

KEYWORDS:
magnet shape, horseshoe magnet, bar magnet, field concentration, lifting power, Maglev

SECTION: SUMMARY
Magnets show consistent behaviors that can be explained by magnetic fields and atomic alignment. A freely suspended magnet aligns North-South because Earth acts like a giant magnet, which is why compasses work. Magnet interaction follows the law of magnetism: like poles repel and unlike poles attract through non-contact force. Magnets can induce temporary magnetism in iron and steel, and magnetic force can pass through non-magnetic materials but be weakened or redirected by magnetic metals. Breaking a magnet does not create single poles; each piece becomes a smaller complete magnet with two poles. Magnets can lose strength through demagnetization caused by heat and repeated shock, so careful handling is important. Magnet shape also affects force distribution, with horseshoe magnets producing concentrated fields useful for strong lifting and advanced engineering applications.
