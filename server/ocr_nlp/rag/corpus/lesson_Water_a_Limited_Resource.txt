SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: WLR_01
NAME: Distribution of Earth’s Water
DEFINITION:
Although Earth is covered mostly by water, only a very small fraction is available for human use.

KEY_POINTS:
- About 97% of Earth’s water is salt water in oceans and seas.
- Only about 3% is fresh water.
- Most fresh water is trapped in glaciers and polar ice caps.
- Less than 1% of total water is available in rivers, lakes, and groundwater for use.

EXAMPLES:
Oceans contain the majority of Earth's water.
Rivers and wells provide only a tiny usable fraction.

KEYWORDS:
water distribution, salt water, fresh water, glaciers, usable water

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: WLR_02
NAME: Water Scarcity
DEFINITION:
Water scarcity occurs when available clean fresh water is not enough to meet the needs of people and ecosystems.

KEY_POINTS:
- Fresh water supply is limited.
- Population growth increases demand.
- Climate change affects rainfall patterns.
- Droughts reduce available water sources.

EXAMPLES:
Water rationing during dry months.
Drying wells during long droughts.

KEYWORDS:
water scarcity, drought, water stress, rationing

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: WLR_03
NAME: Causes of Limited Water Availability
DEFINITION:
Several natural and human factors reduce the amount of usable fresh water.

KEY_POINTS:
- Pollution makes water unsafe.
- Wasteful usage reduces shared supply.
- Climate change alters rainfall.
- Deforestation disrupts water regulation.

EXAMPLES:
Polluted rivers becoming unsafe.
Traditional tanks drying during drought.

KEYWORDS:
pollution, wasteful usage, climate change, deforestation

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: WLR_04
NAME: Water Pollution
DEFINITION:
Water pollution occurs when harmful substances contaminate rivers, lakes, or groundwater, making them unsafe.

KEY_POINTS:
- Industrial waste releases chemicals into rivers.
- Agrochemicals seep into groundwater.
- Plastic waste blocks drains and pollutes lagoons.
- Polluted water reduces usable supply.

EXAMPLES:
Pollution in the Kelani River.
Groundwater issues in North Central Province.

KEYWORDS:
industrial waste, agrochemicals, contamination, plastic pollution

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: WLR_05
NAME: Role of Forests in Water Protection
DEFINITION:
Forests help regulate water by absorbing rain and releasing it slowly into rivers and groundwater.

KEY_POINTS:
- Trees act like natural sponges.
- Reduce floods during heavy rain.
- Maintain river flow during dry seasons.
- Deforestation increases water instability.

EXAMPLES:
Central hill forests supporting river systems.

KEYWORDS:
forests, rainfall absorption, runoff, flood prevention

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: WLR_06
NAME: Water Conservation
DEFINITION:
Water conservation means using water carefully and efficiently to prevent waste.

KEY_POINTS:
- Turn off taps when not in use.
- Fix leaking pipes.
- Use buckets instead of hoses.
- Practice rainwater harvesting.

EXAMPLES:
Collecting rainwater from roofs in dry zones.

KEYWORDS:
water conservation, rainwater harvesting, saving water

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: WLR_07
NAME: Virtual Water
DEFINITION:
Virtual water is the hidden amount of water used to produce goods and food.

KEY_POINTS:
- Water is used in farming and manufacturing.
- Producing cotton clothing requires large water volumes.
- Wasting products wastes water resources.
- Awareness reduces indirect water waste.

EXAMPLES:
About 2,500 liters of water used to produce one cotton t-shirt.

KEYWORDS:
virtual water, hidden water use, production water, sustainability

==================================================
SECTION: RELATION
RELATION_ID: REL_WLR_01
FROM: Pollution
TO: Water Scarcity
EXPLANATION:
Pollution removes clean water from the usable supply, increasing water scarcity.

==================================================
SECTION: RELATION
RELATION_ID: REL_WLR_02
FROM: Deforestation
TO: Water Instability
EXPLANATION:
Cutting forests increases rapid runoff, causing floods and dry river conditions.

==================================================
SECTION: RELATION
RELATION_ID: REL_WLR_03
FROM: Population Growth
TO: Water Stress
EXPLANATION:
As population increases, demand for fresh water rises while supply remains limited.

==================================================
SECTION: EXAMPLE
EXAMPLE_ID: EX_WLR_01
TITLE: The 10-Liter Bucket Model

DESCRIPTION:
If all Earth's water filled a 10-liter bucket, 9.7 liters would be salt water, most fresh water would be trapped in ice, and only about one teaspoon would be available for human use.

==================================================
SECTION: EXAMPLE
EXAMPLE_ID: EX_WLR_02
TITLE: Rainwater Harvesting in Dry Zones

DESCRIPTION:
Families collect rainwater from roofs into tanks to reduce pressure on groundwater supplies.

SECTION: MISCONCEPTION
MISCONCEPTION_ID: MC_WLR_01
STATEMENT:
Because Earth looks blue, water is unlimited.

CORRECTION:
Most water is salt water or frozen, and only a tiny fraction is usable fresh water.

==================================================
SECTION: MISCONCEPTION
MISCONCEPTION_ID: MC_WLR_02
STATEMENT:
Water pollution only affects rivers near cities.

CORRECTION:
Pollution can spread into groundwater and affect rural areas as well.

==================================================
SECTION: MISCONCEPTION
MISCONCEPTION_ID: MC_WLR_03
STATEMENT:
Wasting clothes or food does not affect water supply.

CORRECTION:
Producing goods requires large amounts of water, so waste indirectly wastes water.

SECTION: SUMMARY
Although Earth is covered with water, most of it is salt water or frozen. Only a very small fraction is available for human use. Pollution, deforestation, climate change, and population growth increase water scarcity. Water conservation and responsible habits are essential to protect this limited resource.
