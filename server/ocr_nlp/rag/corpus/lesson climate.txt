SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: CL_01
NAME: Climate
DEFINITION:
Climate is the average weather condition of a place over a long period of time, usually 30 years or more.

KEY_POINTS:
- Weather changes daily.
- Climate describes long-term patterns.
- Climate is more stable than weather.

EXAMPLES:
Rain falling today is weather.
Sri Lanka having rainy months every year is climate.

KEYWORDS:
average weather, long-term, pattern, temperature

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: CL_02
NAME: Weather
DEFINITION:
Weather is the condition of the atmosphere at a specific time and place.

KEY_POINTS:
- Weather changes from hour to hour.
- It includes rain, sunshine, wind, and temperature.
- Weather can be measured daily.

EXAMPLES:
A sunny afternoon.
A rainy morning.

KEYWORDS:
daily change, rain, wind, temperature

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: CL_03
NAME: Factors Affecting Climate
DEFINITION:
Several factors determine the climate of a region.

KEY_POINTS:
- Distance from the equator
- Altitude (height above sea level)
- Distance from the sea
- Rainfall patterns

KEYWORDS:
equator, altitude, sea, rainfall

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: CL_04
NAME: Altitude Effect
DEFINITION:
Temperature decreases as altitude increases.

KEY_POINTS:
- Higher places are cooler.
- Temperature drops about 6.5°C per 1,000 meters.
- Hill country areas are cooler than coastal areas.

EXAMPLES:
Nuwara Eliya is cooler than Colombo.
Mountain climbers wear jackets.

KEYWORDS:
height, cooler, mountain, temperature

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: CL_05
NAME: Ocean Influence
DEFINITION:
The ocean helps control temperature in coastal areas.

KEY_POINTS:
- Water heats up slowly.
- Water cools down slowly.
- Coastal areas have moderate temperatures.

EXAMPLES:
Colombo does not become extremely hot or cold.
Sea breeze cools land during the day.

KEYWORDS:
sea breeze, coastal, moderate temperature

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: CL_06
NAME: Monsoons
DEFINITION:
Monsoons are seasonal winds that bring rain.

KEY_POINTS:
- South-West Monsoon brings rain to the south and west.
- North-East Monsoon brings rain to the north and east.
- Farmers depend on monsoon seasons.

EXAMPLES:
Yala season farming.
Maha season farming.

KEYWORDS:
seasonal wind, rainfall, Yala, Maha

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: CL_07
NAME: Climate Change
DEFINITION:
Climate change is the long-term change in Earth’s climate caused mainly by human activities.

KEY_POINTS:
- Burning fuels increases carbon dioxide.
- Extra greenhouse gases trap more heat.
- Climate change causes floods and droughts.

EXAMPLES:
Unusual heavy rain in Sri Lanka.
Rising sea levels.

KEYWORDS:
greenhouse effect, CO2, global warming

==================================================

SECTION: EXAMPLE
EXAMPLE_ID: EX_CL_01
TITLE: Road Trip from Colombo to Nuwara Eliya

DESCRIPTION:
Colombo is hot and humid.
As you travel uphill, temperature decreases.
Nuwara Eliya is much cooler due to altitude.

==================================================
SECTION: EXAMPLE
EXAMPLE_ID: EX_CL_02
TITLE: Sea Breeze Effect

DESCRIPTION:
Land heats faster than water during the day.
Hot air rises over land.
Cool air from the ocean moves in.
This creates a sea breeze.

==================================================
SECTION: MISCONCEPTION
MISCONCEPTION_ID: MC_CL_01
STATEMENT:
Weather and climate are the same thing.

CORRECTION:
Weather is short-term change, while climate is the long-term pattern.

SECTION: SUMMARY
Climate describes long-term weather patterns of a place. It is influenced by altitude, distance from the equator, rainfall, and proximity to the ocean. Sri Lanka has different climatic zones and depends heavily on monsoons. Climate change is affecting weather patterns worldwide.
