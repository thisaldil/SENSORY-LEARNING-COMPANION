SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: C1
NAME: Electricity Must Be Generated
DEFINITION:
Electricity is produced by converting other forms of energy, not collected directly from nature.

KEY_POINTS:

Electricity generation changes one energy form into electrical energy.
Generation can happen in small devices or large power stations.
Different technologies use different original energy sources.

EXAMPLES:
A torch battery generates a small electric current, while a power station generator produces electricity for many homes.

KEYWORDS:
electricity generation, energy conversion, power station, electrical energy

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: C2
NAME: Major Electricity Sources in Sri Lanka
DEFINITION:
Sri Lanka generates electricity mainly from hydro, thermal, wind, and solar power systems.

KEY_POINTS:

Hydropower uses falling water to rotate turbines at dams such as Victoria and Randenigala.
Thermal power burns coal or fuel oil to produce steam that spins turbines, including at Lakvijaya Norochcholai.
Wind turbines in areas such as Mannar and Puttalam and solar panels convert wind and sunlight into electricity.

EXAMPLES:
Water released from a dam turns a turbine that drives a generator to produce electrical power.

KEYWORDS:
hydroelectric, thermal power, wind power, solar power, Sri Lanka energy

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: C3
NAME: Generator and Electromagnetic Induction
DEFINITION:
Most large-scale generation (except direct solar photovoltaic generation) uses electromagnetic induction, where moving magnetic fields near coils produce electric current.

KEY_POINTS:

Electromagnetic induction was discovered by Michael Faraday.
A generator contains magnets and coils, and relative motion between them creates current.
Hydro, thermal, and wind systems mainly differ in how they spin the generator.

EXAMPLES:
High-pressure steam in a thermal plant rotates a turbine, which spins the generator and induces current.

KEYWORDS:
generator, electromagnetic induction, Faraday, magnet, coil, turbine

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: C4
NAME: Electric Cells and Dynamo Devices
DEFINITION:
Small devices can generate electricity using chemical reactions in cells or mechanical rotation in dynamos.

KEY_POINTS:

Primary cells are non-rechargeable and must be replaced when chemicals are exhausted.
Secondary cells are rechargeable and can be used many times.
A bicycle dynamo converts wheel rotation into electricity for a lamp.

EXAMPLES:
A zinc-carbon wall clock battery is a primary cell, while a mobile phone battery is a rechargeable secondary cell.

KEYWORDS:
electric cell, primary cell, secondary cell, rechargeable battery, bicycle dynamo

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: C5
NAME: Solar Cell Operation (Photovoltaic Effect)
DEFINITION:
Solar cells convert sunlight directly into electricity when photons strike silicon and free electrons, creating electric current.

KEY_POINTS:

Solar cells have no moving parts.
Silicon is a key material used in most solar cells.
Sri Lanka’s strong sunlight near the equator supports solar power potential.

EXAMPLES:
Rooftop solar panels in Sri Lanka generate daytime electricity for homes and schools.

KEYWORDS:
solar cell, photovoltaic effect, silicon, photons, clean energy

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: C6
NAME: Static Electricity by Friction
DEFINITION:
Static electricity is produced when friction causes electrons to transfer between different materials.

KEY_POINTS:

Static electricity often appears as small sparks or "zaps."
It can occur when rubbing a balloon on hair or touching metal after friction.
Lightning is a large natural example of static charge discharge.

EXAMPLES:
A student may feel a small shock when touching a metal handle after walking on a dry surface.

KEYWORDS:
static electricity, friction, electrons, electric discharge, lightning

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: C7
NAME: Transmission, Transformers, and Home Supply
DEFINITION:
After generation, electricity travels through transmission lines at high voltage, then transformers reduce voltage to safer household levels.

KEY_POINTS:

Power stations send electricity over long distances using high-voltage lines.
Transformers lower voltage near towns before household distribution.
Standard household supply in Sri Lanka is about 230V.

EXAMPLES:
Electricity from a distant power plant reaches a local transformer before entering homes and powering appliances.

KEYWORDS:
transmission lines, transformer, voltage, distribution grid, 230V Sri Lanka

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: C8
NAME: Environmental Impact and Energy Mix
DEFINITION:
Each generation method has benefits and environmental costs, so a balanced energy mix is used to improve reliability and reduce harm.

KEY_POINTS:

Coal and oil plants provide dependable power but increase air pollution and global warming.
Hydropower is cleaner during operation, but large dams can affect forests and wildlife habitats.
Solar and wind are clean and renewable, but output depends on sunlight and wind conditions.

EXAMPLES:
Sri Lanka uses multiple sources together so electricity remains available while reducing environmental damage.

KEYWORDS:
environmental impact, energy mix, renewable energy, pollution, sustainability

SECTION: SUMMARY
Electricity is produced by converting other forms of energy using systems from small cells to large generators. In Sri Lanka, major generation methods include hydro, thermal, wind, and solar power. Most large rotating systems use electromagnetic induction, while solar cells generate directly through the photovoltaic effect. Electricity then travels through high-voltage transmission lines and is reduced by transformers to about 230V for safe home use. Different methods have different environmental costs, so Sri Lanka uses an energy mix to balance reliable electricity supply with protection of nature.
