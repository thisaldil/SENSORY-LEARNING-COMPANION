SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: CE_01
NAME: Conservation of Electricity
DEFINITION:
Conservation of electricity means using electrical energy carefully and avoiding unnecessary waste.

KEY_POINTS:
- Electricity is expensive to produce.
- Many power plants burn coal and oil.
- Burning fuels releases carbon dioxide.
- Saving electricity reduces pollution.

EXAMPLES:
Switching off lights when leaving a room.
Using natural sunlight during the day.
Ironing clothes in batches.

KEYWORDS:
save energy, pollution, non-renewable, efficiency

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: CE_02
NAME: Energy Efficiency
DEFINITION:
Energy efficiency means using devices that provide the same service while consuming less electricity.

KEY_POINTS:
- LED bulbs use less power than filament bulbs.
- 5-star appliances use less electricity than 1-star models.
- Efficient devices reduce electricity bills.

EXAMPLES:
Replacing filament bulbs with LED bulbs.
Buying appliances with Energy Star ratings.

KEYWORDS:
LED, rating system, efficiency, sustainability

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: CE_03
NAME: Electric Shock
DEFINITION:
An electric shock occurs when electric current passes through the human body.

KEY_POINTS:
- The human body contains water and salts.
- External current interferes with body’s electrical signals.
- Electric shock can stop the heart.
- Sri Lankan household voltage is about 230V.

EXAMPLES:
Touching a live wire.
Touching a switch with wet hands.

KEYWORDS:
current, voltage, heart rhythm, safety

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: CE_04
NAME: Electrical Overloading
DEFINITION:
Overloading happens when too many appliances draw electricity from one socket.

KEY_POINTS:
- Excess current produces heat.
- Overheated wires can cause fires.
- Avoid using too many devices in one outlet.

EXAMPLES:
Plugging multiple heavy appliances into one extension.
Using damaged wiring.

KEYWORDS:
overheat, fire hazard, current flow

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: CE_05
NAME: Grounding (Earthing)
DEFINITION:
Grounding provides a safe path for excess electricity to flow into the earth.

KEY_POINTS:
- The green/yellow wire is the earth wire.
- It protects people from electric shock.
- It directs stray electricity safely away.

KEYWORDS:
earth wire, protection, safety circuit

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: CE_06
NAME: Safety Devices
DEFINITION:
Safety devices automatically stop electricity flow when danger is detected.

KEY_POINTS:
- A fuse melts if current is too high.
- A circuit breaker trips during overload.
- RCCB detects leakage current.

EXAMPLES:
Fuse wire melting during short circuit.
Trip switch cutting power instantly.

KEYWORDS:
fuse, circuit breaker, RCCB, protection

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: CE_07
NAME: Phantom Power
DEFINITION:
Phantom power is electricity used by devices in standby mode.

KEY_POINTS:
- Standby lights consume electricity.
- Small amounts add up over time.
- Turning off at the socket saves more energy.

EXAMPLES:
Television standby light.
Microwave clock display.

KEYWORDS:
standby, hidden consumption, energy waste

==================================================
SECTION: RELATION
RELATION_ID: REL_CE_01
FROM: Electricity Production
TO: Pollution
EXPLANATION:
Burning coal and oil to generate electricity releases carbon dioxide into the atmosphere.

==================================================
SECTION: RELATION
RELATION_ID: REL_CE_02
FROM: Overloading
TO: Fire Risk
EXPLANATION:
Excess current increases heat, which can ignite surrounding materials.

==================================================
SECTION: RELATION
RELATION_ID: REL_CE_03
FROM: Grounding
TO: Electric Shock Prevention
EXPLANATION:
Grounding directs stray electricity safely into the earth instead of through a person.

==================================================
SECTION: EXAMPLE
EXAMPLE_ID: EX_CE_01
TITLE: Safe Use of Appliances

DESCRIPTION:
Switch off appliances when not in use and unplug chargers to prevent phantom power waste.

==================================================
SECTION: EXAMPLE
EXAMPLE_ID: EX_CE_02
TITLE: Avoiding Overloaded Sockets

DESCRIPTION:
Using separate sockets for heavy appliances like irons and heaters reduces overheating risk.

==================================================
SECTION: EXAMPLE
EXAMPLE_ID: EX_CE_03
TITLE: First Aid for Electric Shock

DESCRIPTION:
Do not touch the victim directly.
Turn off main power immediately.
Use a dry wooden stick to separate the person from the source.
Call emergency number 1990.

==================================================
SECTION: MISCONCEPTION
MISCONCEPTION_ID: MC_CE_01
STATEMENT:
Turning a device off with the remote means it uses no electricity.

CORRECTION:
Devices in standby mode still consume phantom power unless switched off at the wall socket.
