SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: AN_01
NAME: Herbivores
DEFINITION:
Herbivores are animals that feed only on plants or plant products.

KEY_POINTS:
- Eat leaves, fruits, seeds, grass, or nectar.
- Have flat, broad teeth for grinding plant fibers.
- Often have long digestive systems.
- Important as primary consumers in food chains.

EXAMPLES:
Cow, Elephant, Rabbit, Deer, Giraffe.

KEYWORDS:
herbivore, plant eater, grinding teeth, primary consumer

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: AN_02
NAME: Carnivores
DEFINITION:
Carnivores are animals that feed only on the flesh of other animals.

KEY_POINTS:
- Have sharp canines or hooked beaks.
- Possess claws for capturing prey.
- Usually have shorter digestive systems.
- Occupy higher trophic levels in food chains.

EXAMPLES:
Lion, Leopard, Eagle, Crocodile, Shark.

KEYWORDS:
carnivore, meat eater, sharp teeth, predator

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: AN_03
NAME: Omnivores
DEFINITION:
Omnivores are animals that eat both plant and animal matter.

KEY_POINTS:
- Flexible diet supports survival in varied environments.
- Teeth adapted for both cutting and grinding.
- Can act as both primary and secondary consumers.

EXAMPLES:
Humans, Crows, Pigs, Bears, Monkeys.

KEYWORDS:
omnivore, mixed diet, adaptable feeder

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: AN_04
NAME: Scavengers
DEFINITION:
Scavengers are animals that feed on the remains of dead animals.

KEY_POINTS:
- Help clean the environment.
- Reduce spread of disease.
- Part of nutrient recycling in ecosystems.
- Often have strong beaks or jaws.

EXAMPLES:
Vulture, Hyena, Jackal, certain beetles.

KEYWORDS:
scavenger, carrion, environmental cleaning

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: AN_05
NAME: Specialized Feeding Types
DEFINITION:
Some animals have specialized feeding habits within broader nutrition groups.

KEY_POINTS:
- Frugivores eat mainly fruits.
- Nectarivores feed on nectar using long mouthparts.
- Insectivores eat insects.
- Filter feeders strain small organisms from water.

EXAMPLES:
Fruit Bat (frugivore).
Sunbird (nectarivore).
Gecko (insectivore).
Blue Whale (filter feeder).

KEYWORDS:
frugivore, nectarivore, insectivore, filter feeder

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: AN_06
NAME: Physical Adaptations for Feeding
DEFINITION:
Animals develop physical structures suited to their mode of nutrition.

KEY_POINTS:
- Bird beaks vary according to diet.
- Herbivores have long intestines.
- Carnivores have strong jaws and claws.
- Baleen plates filter food in whales.

EXAMPLES:
Sunbird beak for nectar.
Eagle beak for tearing meat.
Cow digestive system for grass digestion.

KEYWORDS:
adaptation, beak structure, digestive system, form and function

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: AN_07
NAME: Symbiotic Nutrition
DEFINITION:
Symbiotic nutrition occurs when animals gain food through cooperative relationships with other organisms.

KEY_POINTS:
- Mutualism benefits both organisms.
- One species may provide food cleaning services.
- Strengthens ecosystem relationships.

EXAMPLES:
Oxpecker bird eating ticks from buffalo.

KEYWORDS:
symbiosis, mutualism, cooperation

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: AN_08
NAME: Parasitic Nutrition
DEFINITION:
Parasitic nutrition occurs when one organism lives on or inside another and obtains nutrients at the host’s expense.

KEY_POINTS:
- Ectoparasites live outside the body.
- Endoparasites live inside the body.
- Parasites have hooks or suckers for attachment.
- Can harm the host.

EXAMPLES:
Ticks, leeches, tapeworms.

KEYWORDS:
parasite, ectoparasite, endoparasite, host

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: AN_09
NAME: Ecological Role of Scavengers and Decomposers
DEFINITION:
Scavengers and decomposers recycle nutrients and maintain ecosystem balance.

KEY_POINTS:
- Scavengers consume dead animals.
- Decomposers break down remains into nutrients.
- Nutrients return to soil for plant growth.
- Supports food chain continuity.

EXAMPLES:
Vultures cleaning carcasses.
Fungi decomposing remains.

KEYWORDS:
nutrient cycle, decomposer, ecosystem balance

SECTION: MISCONCEPTION
MISCONCEPTION_ID: MC_AN_02
STATEMENT:
Scavengers are harmful animals.

CORRECTION:
Scavengers help prevent disease and clean the environment.

==================================================
SECTION: MISCONCEPTION
MISCONCEPTION_ID: MC_AN_03
STATEMENT:
Omnivores eat equal amounts of plants and meat.

CORRECTION:
Omnivores may vary their diet depending on availability.

- Classify animals as herbivores, carnivores, omnivores, or scavengers.

==================================================
SECTION: SUMMARY
Animals are classified by their mode of nutrition into herbivores, carnivores, omnivores, and scavengers. Specialized feeding habits such as nectar feeding, insect feeding, and filter feeding show how form supports function. Symbiotic and parasitic relationships demonstrate complex nutritional strategies. Scavengers and decomposers play essential roles in recycling nutrients and maintaining ecosystem balance.
