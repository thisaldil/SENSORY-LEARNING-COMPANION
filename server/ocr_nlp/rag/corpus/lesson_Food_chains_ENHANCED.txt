SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: FC_01
NAME: Food Chain
DEFINITION:
A food chain is a simple pathway that shows how energy moves from one living organism to another.

KEY_POINTS:
- Energy starts from the Sun.
- Plants capture sunlight.
- Animals obtain energy by eating plants or other animals.
- Energy flows in one direction only.

KEYWORDS:
energy flow, pathway, organisms, sunlight

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: FC_02
NAME: Producer
DEFINITION:
A producer is a living organism that makes its own food using sunlight through photosynthesis.

KEY_POINTS:
- Producers are always the first step in a food chain.
- They convert solar energy into chemical energy (food).
- Without producers, food chains cannot exist.

EXAMPLES:
Grass
Rice plant
Trees
Algae

KEYWORDS:
photosynthesis, plant, sunlight, first level

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: FC_03
NAME: Primary Consumer
DEFINITION:
A primary consumer is an animal that eats plants.

KEY_POINTS:
- Also called herbivores.
- They receive energy directly from producers.
- They form the second trophic level.

EXAMPLES:
Cow
Goat
Grasshopper
Rat (paddy field)

KEYWORDS:
herbivore, plant eater, second level

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: FC_04
NAME: Secondary Consumer
DEFINITION:
A secondary consumer is an animal that eats primary consumers.

KEY_POINTS:
- Usually small carnivores.
- They receive less energy than primary consumers.
- They form the third trophic level.

EXAMPLES:
Snake
Cat
Eagle
Owl

KEYWORDS:
carnivore, predator, third level

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: FC_05
NAME: Tertiary Consumer
DEFINITION:
A tertiary consumer is a top predator that eats other carnivores.

KEY_POINTS:
- Usually at the top of the food chain.
- Very little energy reaches this level.
- Few organisms exist at this level.

EXAMPLES:
Tiger
Lion
Hawk
Crocodile
Human

KEYWORDS:
top predator, apex, highest level

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: FC_06
NAME: Decomposer
DEFINITION:
A decomposer is an organism that breaks down dead plants and animals and returns nutrients to the soil.

KEY_POINTS:
- Decomposers recycle nutrients.
- They help plants grow again.
- They complete the energy and nutrient cycle.

EXAMPLES:
Bacteria
Fungi
Worms
Termites

KEYWORDS:
recycle, nutrients, soil, breakdown

==================================================
SECTION: CORE_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: FC_07
NAME: Trophic Level
DEFINITION:
A trophic level is each step in a food chain.

LEVELS:
Level 1 – Producer
Level 2 – Primary Consumer
Level 3 – Secondary Consumer
Level 4 – Tertiary Consumer

ENERGY_RULE:
Only about 10 percent of energy passes to the next level.

KEYWORDS:
energy pyramid, levels, energy loss

==================================================
SECTION: RELATION
RELATION_ID: REL_01
FROM: Sun
TO: Producer
EXPLANATION:
Producers capture solar energy from the Sun and convert it into food.

==================================================
SECTION: RELATION
RELATION_ID: REL_02
FROM: Producer
TO: Primary Consumer
EXPLANATION:
Primary consumers obtain stored plant energy by eating producers.

==================================================
SECTION: RELATION
RELATION_ID: REL_03
FROM: Primary Consumer
TO: Secondary Consumer
EXPLANATION:
Secondary consumers obtain energy by eating herbivores.

==================================================
SECTION: RELATION
RELATION_ID: REL_04
FROM: All Levels
TO: Decomposer
EXPLANATION:
When organisms die, decomposers break them down and recycle nutrients.

==================================================
SECTION: EXAMPLE
EXAMPLE_ID: EX_01
TITLE: Paddy Field Food Chain (Sri Lanka)

CHAIN:
Rice Plant → Rat → Cobra → Peacock

EXPLANATION:
The rice plant captures sunlight.
The rat eats rice grains.
The cobra eats the rat.
The peacock eats the cobra.

==================================================
SECTION: EXAMPLE
EXAMPLE_ID: EX_02
TITLE: Ocean Food Chain

CHAIN:
Phytoplankton → Zooplankton → Small Fish → Tuna

EXPLANATION:
Tiny sea plants capture sunlight.
Small sea animals eat them.
Fish eat the small animals.
Large fish eat smaller fish.

==================================================
SECTION: MISCONCEPTION
MISCONCEPTION_ID: MC_01
STATEMENT:
The arrow in a food chain shows who eats whom.

CORRECTION:
The arrow shows the direction of energy flow, not the direction of eating.

==================================================
SECTION: ADVANCED_CONCEPT
CONCEPT_ID: FC_08
NAME: Bio-magnification
DEFINITION:
Bio-magnification is the increase in concentration of pollutants as they move up the food chain.

EXPLANATION:
Small organisms absorb small amounts of pollution.
Predators eat many smaller organisms.
Pollution accumulates at higher levels.

==================================================
SECTION: SCENE_DESCRIPTION
SCENE_ID: SC_01
TITLE: Energy Journey
DESCRIPTION:
Sunlight shines on a rice field.
Energy glows as it enters green leaves.
Energy transfers from rice to rat.
Energy transfers from rat to cobra.
Energy transfers from cobra to peacock.

==================================================
SECTION: LEARNING_OBJECTIVES
- Define a food chain.
- Identify producers and consumers.
- Explain trophic levels.
- Describe energy loss between levels.
- Recognize the role of decomposers.
- Explain why balance is important.

==================================================
SECTION: SUMMARY
A food chain shows how energy moves from the Sun to plants and then to animals. Each step receives less energy than the one before it. Decomposers recycle nutrients and maintain balance in ecosystems.
